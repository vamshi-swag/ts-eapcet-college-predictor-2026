How to Use MarsMate EAPCET

Complete step-by-step guide — from profile to seat allotment

1

Fill Your Student Profile Sidebar

Everything starts from the sidebar on the left. This is your profile — the predictor, option form, and fee calculator all use it automatically.

  • 🎯
    Rank or Marks mode: Toggle between "Use Rank" (enter your EAPCET rank directly) and "Use Marks" (enter your score out of 160 — your rank is auto-predicted from the table).
  • 👤
    Gender / Pool: General shows all colleges, Boys shows co-ed only, Girls shows all colleges plus women-only institutions. With Girls selected, you can also "Prioritize Women's Colleges First."
  • 📋
    Reservation Category: Choose your caste certificate category (OC, EWS, BC-A to BC-E, SC Group I–III, ST). This controls which cutoffs are used for predictions.
  • 📍
    Region Eligibility: OU Area if you studied in Hyderabad/Rangareddy/Medchal district (Osmania local area). Non-Local otherwise. OU-local students get 85% of seats reserved.
  • 💰
    Annual Family Income: Determines your fee reimbursement eligibility. Below ₹2L qualifies for the most benefits.
  • 📚
    TS Study Record: Check this if you've studied in Telangana for at least 4 of the last 7 years — required for fee reimbursement.
💡 The Fee Reimbursement badge at the bottom of the sidebar updates instantly as you change income and category — check it to know if you're eligible for ₹35,000–₹1,15,000/year.
2

Understand the College Predictor College Predictor Tab

The predictor shows colleges and branches where your rank has a realistic chance, using 2025 cutoffs from all 3 phases.

  • 🟢
    Safe Pick: Your rank is well below the cutoff — very high probability of admission in Phase 1 itself.
  • 🔵
    Good Bet: Your rank is at or near the cutoff — strong probability, especially across multiple phases.
  • 🟡
    Borderline: Your rank is slightly above the cutoff — possible if cutoffs ease in Phase 2 or Final Phase.
  • 🔴
    Reach: Cutoff is well below your rank — unlikely unless significant expansion happens.
  • ↑↓→
    Trend arrow next to cutoffs: ↑ means cutoff got harder (Phase 1 → Final); ↓ means easier; → means stable. Shows how competitive a seat became over phases.
  • ⭐🥇🥈🥉
    Quality tiers (Recommended sort): Elite, Premier, Good, Standard — ranked by placement outcomes (35%), NIRF rank (25%), NAAC grade (20%), and college type (10%).
💡 Use Rank Buffer (slider in the filter bar) to add ±N to your rank and see how results change — useful for planning if your 2026 rank shifts slightly.
3

Filter, Sort, and Narrow Results College Predictor Tab

Use the filter bar above the results to drill down to exactly what you want.

  • 🔍
    Search box: Search by college name, EAPCET code (e.g., CBIT), place, or district.
  • 📊
    Sort By: Switch between Recommended (default — best ROI first), Admission Chance, Tuition Fee (lowest first), Avg Package (highest first), NAAC Grade, or Estd. Year.
  • 🌿
    Branch filter: Click to pick specific branches (CSE, ECE, EEE…). Selected branches appear as draggable chips — drag them to set your priority order.
  • 📍
    District filter: Narrow to one or more districts (e.g., Hyderabad, Rangareddy, Warangal).
  • 🏛
    Type / Affiliation filters: Government vs Private vs Deemed; JNTUH vs OU vs KU vs Deemed.
  • 🛏
    Hostel / NAAC checkboxes: Quick toggle for hostels and NAAC A+ or above.
  • 🔀
    Preference Order toggle: College First groups results by college (top college → all your branches). Branch First groups by branch (top branch → all matching colleges). Both modes respect your branch priority order.
  • 📋📊
    Cards / Table toggle: Switch between visual college cards and a compact sortable table view.
💡 Click "Add All Filtered to Option Form" or "Sync to Option Form" in the filter bar to push your entire filtered list directly into the Option Form — a big time-saver.
4

Build Your Option Form Option Form Tab

The Option Form is your ranked preference list — the actual order you'll submit during TSCHE counselling. Priority #1 at the top means it's your most wanted seat.

  • ➕
    Add branches: Search the left panel for any college+branch combination. Click the "+" button to add it to your list on the right.
  • 🃏
    From Predictor cards: Click "Add Options" on any college card to add all its branches (sorted by cutoff) in one click.
  • ↕
    Reorder by drag: Drag items in the right panel up or down to set your priority. TSCHE will assign you the highest option in this list that your rank qualifies for.
  • 🗑
    Remove an option: Click the × button on any row to remove it from your list.
  • 🖨
    Print / Share: Use the Print button to get a printable copy. Use Share to send your option form via WhatsApp to parents or counsellors.
⚠️ Important: Always put your dream college+branch at position #1. Include 50–100 options covering Safe, Good Bet, and Borderline picks — the more options you enter, the safer you are.
5

Simulate Your Allotment Option Form Tab

Once your option list is ready, simulate what seat TSCHE is most likely to assign you.

  • ▶
    Click "Simulate Allotment" in the Option Form tab — the system scans your list top to bottom and returns the first seat where your rank is below or near the 2025 cutoff.
  • 📊
    Phase-wise probability is shown for each branch: Phase 1, Phase 2, and Final Phase chances are color-coded (High / Medium / Low / Very Low).
  • 🔁
    Adjust and retry: If you don't like the simulated result, rearrange your list or add more options and simulate again.
💡 The simulation uses 2025 Final Phase cutoffs as a baseline. Real 2026 cutoffs may shift — treat this as a planning tool, not a guaranteed prediction.
6

Compare Colleges Side-by-Side Compare Tab

Use the Compare tab to place up to 4 colleges side by side and make informed decisions.

  • ⊟
    Add to compare: In the College Predictor, click the compare icon (⊟) on any college card. The badge on the Compare tab shows how many are selected.
  • 📋
    Comparison table covers: Location, college type, affiliation, NAAC grade, founding year, hostel availability, 1-year and total B.Tech fee, average placement, highest package, top recruiters, and available branches.
  • ✕
    Remove a college: Click "Remove" under a college header in the compare table to deselect it.
7

Use the Counselling Tab Counselling Tab

The Counselling tab has six sub-sections covering everything you need from dates to documents to strategy.

  • 📅
    Timeline: Key dates for Phase 1, Phase 2, and Final Phase — slot booking, option form entry, allotment, and reporting deadlines.
  • ✅
    Checklist: Full list of documents needed for Helpline Centre (HLC) certificate verification. Check items off as you prepare. Click Share to send the list via WhatsApp.
  • 💰
    Fee Calculator: Enter your rank, category, income, and college type to get an exact eligibility result and a full annual budget breakdown (tuition + hostel + mess + extras).
  • 🎯
    Phase Strategy: Enter your rank in the sidebar first — then this section gives you personalized advice for each counselling phase based on your rank tier.
  • 📋
    After Allotment: Step-by-step guide on what to do after you receive a seat — reporting, fee payment, uploading documents, and avoiding seat cancellation.
  • ℹ️
    Info Hub: Official links (TSCHE, TSSP, NAAC), FAQ, and key eligibility requirements at a glance.
✦

Key Tips & Reminders

📅 2025 cutoffs are the baseline

All predictions use 2025 Final Phase data. 2026 cutoffs will vary. Use this tool for planning, not as an official prediction.

📝 Enter options for all 3 phases

Don't stop after Phase 1. Cutoffs usually ease in Phase 2 and Final Phase — better seats may open up.

💾 Your data is auto-saved

Your option form and checklist are saved in your browser automatically. Revisit anytime — no login needed.

🔗 Share your search easily

Click the Share button in the Predictor tab to copy a link encoding your rank, category, and filters. Send it to parents or counsellors.

💸 Check fee reimbursement first

If your income is below ₹2L and you studied in TS, you likely qualify for ₹35,000–₹1,15,000/year off your tuition. Check the Counselling → Fee Calculator.

🏛 Prefer Government colleges

Government / constituent colleges (UCE, JNTUH) have much lower fees and fee reimbursement applies fully. Keep them high in your option form.
